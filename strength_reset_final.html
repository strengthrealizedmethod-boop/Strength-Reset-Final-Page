Success Tips

Make it work in real life

01
Prep proteins on Sunday
Cook a batch of chicken, boil eggs, and portion Greek yogurt. When protein is ready, hitting your daily target becomes effortless.
02
Swap freely, not randomly
Swap protein for protein, carb for carb. Do not like cod? Use salmon. Do not like quinoa? Use brown rice. Keep the structure.
03
Eat more if you train hard
These calorie ranges are a guide, not a ceiling. If you lift weights, you may need 150 to 250 extra calories. Undereating breaks metabolism.
04
Do not fear healthy fats
Fat is not the enemy after 40. Your hormones need it. Removing avocado or nuts to save calories backfires every time.
05
Drink water before every meal
Start every meal with a glass of water. It improves digestion, reduces bloating, and helps you tell genuine hunger from dehydration.
06
Progress is the goal
Day 6 is intentionally flexible. One off-plan meal does not derail a week. It is the other 20 meals that count. Keep going.

Nutrition Philosophy

What this plan stands for

Six principles that make this different from every other nutrition plan you have tried.

Protein at every meal

Every meal and snack has a protein anchor. Keeps you full, preserves muscle, and stabilises blood sugar. The trifecta after 40.

No ultra-processed foods

If it comes in a packet with 20 ingredients you cannot pronounce, skip it this week. Whole foods reduce inflammation and support gut health.

Hydration is non-negotiable

Aim for 2 to 3 litres of water daily. Add electrolytes if you are training. Dehydration mimics hunger and tanks your energy.

Eat within 30 minutes of training

Protein plus carbs within 30 minutes of lifting maximises muscle protein synthesis after 40.

Do not fear healthy fats

Avocado, olive oil, salmon, nuts. Healthy fats support hormone production including oestrogen and reduce systemic inflammation.

Eat before bed if hungry

A small protein snack before bed supports overnight muscle recovery. Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, or casein. Do not starve yourself.
